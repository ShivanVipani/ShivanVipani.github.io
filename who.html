Topic 1: Mental Health

     The first topic of focus will bring nations together to discuss the definition and value of mental health, as well as the possible treatments and protocol for dealing with mental illness. According to the World Health Organization, there are over 200 current mental illnesses. Still, few countries currently have a legal framework that satisfactorily protects the rights of people with mental disorders. Furthermore, many nations do not provide the care that those with serious mental illnesses (SMIs) may require. This lack of support is a potential result of the stigma surrounding mental illness in many countries, which leads to discrimination of individuals. There is also a widespread belief that such illnesses cannot be treated, unlike other more visible maladies, those afflicted face a devastating situation of neglect. The committee will be discussing whether or not mental health should be treated as other types of health, and, if so, what can be done to improve the prevention and treatment of SMIs.


Topic 2: Embryo Selection

     The second topic will discuss embryo selection- the modification of the human fetus' genome to specially select traits that would be desired- and its possible ramifications to global health. The development of genetically modified organisms (GMOs) has led to many ethical and ecological concerns. While GMOs can prove to be beneficial, many people fear the genetic modification of plants and animals. Given these predisposed fears, it comes to no surprise that the modification of the human genome is among the most heated debates in the scientific and international community. Many countries are concerned about "designer babies" and the ethical concerns that surround them, including the possibility of eugenics dominating nations. Weigh the benefits and detriments of embryo selection against each other, with a strong regard for your country's distinct view over the countroversial dispute.
