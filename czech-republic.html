Topic Brief: Side of the Czech Republic

     Three years after the fall of the Czechoslovakian Communist Party, determined by the collapsing system of Eastern European communism, the debate of separation was roaring. The self-determined split between the Czech Republic and Slovakia had arisen before as the Czech Socialist Republic and the Slovak Socialist Republic in 1969 within the framework of Czechoslovak federalisation.Without the force of a single party state, nationalism came to play an important role in politics, particularly in the election of prominent state officials.
     You, as a member of the Czech delegation, are to negotiate for the best possible terms of separation for the future of the Czech Republic. It is up to your delegation to make important policy decisions on legal aspects of the separation such as territory, division of national property, currency division, and participation in international law. All of this committee’s actions will have a direct result on the economy of the fledgling nation, rights of citizenship, and future diplomatic relations with the newly created Slovak Republic.
     The future of the Czech Republic lies in your hands. In any case that the separation of the nation ensues, another future will be designed and history will written.
