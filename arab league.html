Topic 1: Fallout of the Arab Spring

     In 2010, a wave of demonstrations calling for revolution and change dubbed the "Arab Spring" rattled the Arab world in many ways; most notably, it created large-scale strife and struggle dubbed the "Arab Winter" that lasted for years to come . Starting with the Tunisian Revolution, the movement was a response to numerous instances of human rights violations, authoritarianism, and economic turmoil. This movement championed democracy, free elections, and human rights. Ultimately, the Arab Spring resulted in the Northern Mali conflict and the overthrow of the Tunisian, Libyan, Egyptian, and Yemeni governments as well as many other drastic events. Several vulnerable states have taken measures to counteract possible Arab Spring events in their respective countries, and other states have used force to crush the impending threats to their governments. As members of the Arab league, how will you work together to maintain order and peace in this time of turmoil? How will you respond to the Arab Spring movement's demands, and how will you respond to the demands of the Arab governments simultaneously? What will become of the Arab world?


Topic 2: Climate Change and its Impact on Settlement

     The rapid industrialization and advancement of mankind has brought with it a concerning topic: climate change. The Middle East and North African region is one of the most vulnerable areas in the world to climate change, as it is one of the world's most water-scarce and dry regions. Much of the Arab world is dependent on climate-sensitive agriculture and holds much of its population and economic potential in flood-prone coastal cities. Despite increased attention to climate change recently and the World Bank's allocation of an "Adaptation Fund" to help adapt against the effects of climate change, global temperatures continue to increase and water is expected to become scarce in the next several decades. More pressure is being placed on groundwater extraction, pulling more water from aquifers than can be recharged. The drainage of aquifers can bring infrastructural damage and surface depression, and recent research has correlated earthquakes and aquifer depletion. The decreasing availability of water and pressure to use new sources makes settlement increasingly difficult, as an increasing population will put a strain on water distribution. As the Arab League, what compromises and accommodations will you propose to make settlement and advancement feasible? How will you address climate change? How will climate change affect settlement and how will the Arab world respond?
