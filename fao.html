Topic 1: Increasing Food Productivity for a Growing Population

     The world population is projected to increase to more than 9 billion people by the year 2050. By that time, global food production rates will need to increase by 70% and accommodate for 2 billion more people. Both rural and urban areas will be subjected to food deficits well into the 21st century. As members of the FAO it will be up to you to determine new and sustainable methods for growing crops to ensure that we have enough food to survive. Some promising advances have been made in the fields of GMO's, irrigation techniques, pasture productivity, and general farm land sustainability. You must use your research and collaborate to save the world from this impending humanitarian catastrophe.


Topic 2: The Food Industrial Complex

     Green does not always equal green - This is the fundamental fallacy in the global food industry. Growing broccoli and lettuce is not as profitable as growing the grains that make the high fructose corn syrup in coke, or the oil that is used to make french fries. “Big Ag” groups use their substantial margins to shove good-intentioned farmers out of the way, giving them a heft that healthy food producers can’t match. These organizations use their lobbying powers to prioritize their bottom line budgets over public health. As concerned diplomats, it will be up to this committee to decide if the unchecked power of these companies continues to grow, or is monitored to ensure a healthier, safer future for generations to come.
