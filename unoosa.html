Topic 1: Weaponization of the Moon

     “The sky calls to us; If we do not destroy ourselves, we will one day venture to the stars.” - Carl Sagan. The United Nations Office for Outer Space Affairs is the international manifestation of this message and seeks to establish and promote global cooperation in the peaceful use and exploration of space. However, during the Cold War, the United States and the Soviet Union scrambled to project their hegemony throughout the world and even beyond it. Several projects arose during this era that targeted the Moon as a major base of military operations. The Moon provides extraordinary advantages. From launching pads for weapons of mass destruction to global reconnaissance, there is no doubt that if a nation dominates the Moon, they dominate the Earth. These plans for galactic domination have always been a far-fetched ideal of the future, but for no longer shall they remain that way. Welcome to the year 2050. The weaponization of the Moon is no longer the premise of science fiction films, but a dangerous and real possibility. A possibility that threatens the livelihood and unity of not only a country but the entire world. This committee must plan for the future while remembering the past, focusing on the intricacies of regulating such a course of action. Delegates will decide on the destiny of the United Nation’s actions in outer space and draft landmark resolutions that will influence international space affairs in the forthcoming years.


Topic 2: Territorial and Resource Rights

     Space: The Final Frontier. With the advent of new technologies in the year 2050, outer space provides the human race with endless possibilities. As a result, space has become more accessible and open to Earth’s citizens. Therefore, territorial and resource rights are a critical issue in determining the future of our activities in space. The appeal of resources and territory in outer space is one that cannot be ignored by the international community. Cosmic property and capital can yield incredible amounts of profit and is now a realistic economic venture. Territorial claims in space are an additional escalating issue. The benefits garnered by owning extraterrestrial real estate are substantial. Delegates, it is now your duty to unravel the future of extraterrestrial resource exploitation and territorial claims. This committee will emphasize the debate of sovereignty in space, the future of cosmic resource capital, and the drafting of progressive resolutions that will serve as the guidelines of the Final Frontier.
