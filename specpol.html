Topic 1: The Question of Kurdistan

     Imagine living in this world without a place to call home. For decades, Kurds have struggled without their own homeland. The majority of ethnic Kurds live in Turkey, Iran, Iraq and Syria, where attempts to establish their own government has been met with staunch resistance. Anyone who strove for self-determination or liberty was squelched with opposition. Desperate for freedom and looking for a solution to this issue, the Kurds formed the Kurdish Workers Party in 1978. The party turned to violent protest in the coming decades due to years of inaction and little progress. Meanwhile in Turkey, Kurds were told to assimilate. By banning the speaking and teaching of Kurdish and forcing many to exile Turkey has replied to the violence with its own counterattack. Despite this, many Kurds have criticised the PKK for indiscriminate attacks on Turkish civilians in the past, which has resulted in injury, and also death. In Iran, Syria and Iraq, systematic discrimination and violence against Kurds and their practices ensues to this day. The Al-Qamishli massacre of 2004 is one such example. Amidst violence and anger from both sides, how can this issue be solved? What are feasible and novel solutions? It’s about time the question of kurdistan was answered.


Topic 2: Self Determination in Western Sahara

     Called Africa’s last colony, Western Sahara has tried to push for autonomy and self government for years now. Surrounded by Mauritania and Algeria, and claimed by Morocco, Western Sahara is in dire need of liberation. After decades of sometimes violent but mostly peaceful protest, the Polisario Front (Western Sahara’s Liberation Movement) negotiated a deal with the UN. A national referendum was promised after a ceasefire in 1991, in which the population could vote for complete independence or integration with Morocco However the plan stalled when Morocco and Polisario disagreed over who was entitled to vote. To this day, the issue is at a stalemate with neither side backing down. Western Sahara is a mineral rich and naturally resource filled territory. The economic potential is significant, attesting the the difficulty Morocco and other countries have with its independence. The fight rages on among these powers for control of Western Sahara and its Sahrawi people. What can SPECPOL do to break this stalemate? How can diplomacy be achieved? Self Determination isn’t a privilege for few. It’s a right for all.
