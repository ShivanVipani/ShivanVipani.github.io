Topic 1: Biological Warfare

     Throughout the course of history, biological weapons have been used as effective methods of warfare. Many countries, during times of war, utilize biological weapons as a means of gaining strategic advantages, either through the threat or deployment of these weapons. This form of warfare includes the use of microorganisms and biological toxins as weapons of war, which often leads to devastating effects because these agents can target entire populations rather than a single individual. In more recent times, however, the use of biological weapons has become an issue of paramount concern for the international community. Biological warfare rose to prominence during World War I and World War II when infectious agents were introduced by Germany and Japan to be used in combat. The manufacturing of biological weapons came to a head during the Cold War, and soon after the Biological Weapons Convention (BWC) outlawed the mass production and use of biological weapons. Despite the profusion of actions taken by the international community to halt the use of these weapons, many nations to this day continue their research and development of biological agents to use in warfare, posing a significant threat to countries around the world.


Topic 2: Effects of Foreign Bases in Sovereign States

     In recent decades, foreign military bases have become a prominent topic of debate, creating opposition between nationalists and anti-militarists in nations around the world. These military bases may be established for a myriad of reasons; however, they usually revolve around strategic and logistic purposes. These bases are controversial because of their impact on the concept of national sovereignty. There are two main issues regarding foreign military bases: purpose and location. Firstly, hese bases are integral to a nation’s war strategies. As a result, these bases destabilize the region through their proliferation of weapons and military. Secondly, these bases cause local social and environmental problems. These military bases take up resources and facilitate pollution. Often times, the soldiers themselves contribute to local unrest because of the crimes they commit. This multifaceted topic seeks to focus on how the spread of foreign military bases across the world impacts international stability and host nations, and how the global community can deal with the problems created by these bases.
