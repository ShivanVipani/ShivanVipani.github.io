Topic 1: Internally Displaced Persons and Rebel Groups

     Internally Displaced Persons are people who are forced to flee their homes but remain within their borders. Wars and battles of the modern age display repercussions on the global scale. IDP's are similar to refugees but display one simple difference: they are stranded within the confines of their own home with a lack of infrastructure. In Africa's most affected regions, rebel groups dominate and instigate the situation of IDPs. Groups such as the Boko Haram, Sudan Revolutionary Front, and Lord's Resistance Army destroy property and proper infrastructure. The IDPs are not classified as true refugees, and, as a result, they receive little international attention. The responsibilities of this committee lie in the protection of IDPs, both in short-term aid and long-term solutions, as well as the development of strategies to rid Africa of the rebel groups that cause this.


Topic 2: Economic Development

     Africa is notoriously known as one of the poorest and most underdeveloped continents in the world. Population growth and corruption only obstruct the effort for economic success. The African economy relies on World Bank Structural Adjustment Programs and IMF cash transfers to sustain itself. These programs, however, only provide African communities with a base for growth and development. Lack of infrastructure, such as broken highways, paved streets, and food and water access, continue to instigate the issue. Trade policy throughout Africa prevents cohesiveness and successful development. With a GDP growth of 5.16% and with 36.2% of people living with US$1 per day, Africa's struggling economy requires help on a global scale.
