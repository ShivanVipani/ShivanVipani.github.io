Topic Brief: Side of Slovakia

     Czechoslovakia had a difficult history. Declaring independence from Austria-Hungary in the aftermath of World War I, it endured both direct and indirect influence from Nazi Germany and, later, the Soviets. Surviving all of these crises, the end of this nation came in 1993, two years after the fall of the Soviet Union and the end of the Cold War.
     As a delegate from the Slovakian side, you must work tirelessly to ensure that your country’s interests are met in this committee. You will be discussing the legal, economic, political, and social consequences that will surely result from this division. You must not let the more wealthy Czech state dictate the terms; it is up to you to limit the fallout from this difficult division.
     The future of Slovakia is in your hands.
