Topic 1: Policies Towards Curbing Pollution

     In the past few decades, pollution and the need for awareness of its effects have increased dramatically. Pollution is caused by a number of things, whether it be transportation, industry or littering. Pollutants in the air cause numerous health issues not only for humans, but for plants and animals as well. As climate change becomes a growing problem, there is much argument but no action. Many governments ignore climate change and don’t control large corporations out of fear of losing economic power; this mindset is detrimental to the environment as a whole, which has been shown in the melting of the ice caps, decrease in air quality, and lack of clean drinking water. However, many countries are making changes to decrease pollution in the forms of carbon emission regulations, reduction of littering, and increases in education about the issue. The problem will persist if there are no plans or actions taken, which begs the question: how will this committee create policies specific enough to efficiently curb pollution?


Topic 2: The Harm of Fracking

     Hydraulic fracturing, or fracking, is the act of drilling down into the ground and pumping high pressure slick water to open up fissures in shale rock, releasing natural gas and oils. Fracking allows drilling firms to accquire hard-to-reach fuels, increasing domestic oil production and lowering gas prices. While this method has changed the energy industry for the better, it has also caused major environmental concerns. Not only does fracking require massive amounts of water but it contaminates groundwater surrounding the fracking site and other connected bodies of water with potential carncinogens. Fracking is additionally believed to hold the power to cause earth tremors by disrupting the earth’s natural layering. Recently, there many protests have risen up against the use of fracking because of its supposed encouragement of dependence on fossil fuels instead of the search for renewable energy. With high economic benefit from fracking comes high environmental damage as well. For this committee session, delegates are expected to discuss the positives and negatives of fracking and create regulations for how to proceed in the future.
